Guided Cloze (B2–C1) – Traditions Around the World

Every country has its own customs and traditions that have been passed down from one (1) ______ to another. Some of them are (2) ______, dating back hundreds or even thousands of years, while others have developed more recently. Although cultures differ greatly, many societies share (3) ______ belief that traditions help people strengthen their sense of identity.

In many villages, local people gather in (4) ______ communal house during important festivals. These celebrations are often held to (5) ______ historical events or honour respected ancestors. Visitors can also admire impressive (6) ______, such as temples, castles and statues, which remind people of their rich cultural heritage.

Some traditions are so (7) ______ that they remain almost unchanged despite modern influences. Governments and local organisations often work together to (8) ______ these customs because they attract tourists and preserve national identity.

Nowadays, however, life has become more (9) ______. Young people are busier than previous generations, and technology tends to (10) ______ much of their free time. As a result, many families have fewer opportunities to meet (11) ______, making it harder to pass on cultural values naturally.

Travelling abroad also allows people to (12) ______ different ways of life. For example, visitors to Britain often enjoy trying (13) ______ fish and chips from a local (14) ______ shop, while tourists in Scotland may visit (15) ______ ancient castle that has stood for centuries.

Whether customs are (16) ______ or secular, they continue to connect communities and remind people of where they come from.
