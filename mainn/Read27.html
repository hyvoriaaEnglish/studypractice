Market Segmentation in Business

Market segmentation is the process of dividing a broad market into smaller groups of consumers who share similar characteristics, needs, or purchasing behaviors. Rather than treating all customers as identical, businesses identify specific segments based on factors such as age, income, lifestyle, location, or interests. This approach enables companies to design products and marketing strategies that better address the preferences of each group. As a result, firms can allocate resources more efficiently and increase the likelihood of attracting potential customers.

However, effective segmentation requires more than simply grouping consumers according to demographic characteristics. A segment must also be measurable, accessible, substantial, and responsive to marketing efforts. If a target group is too small or difficult to reach, focusing on it may not be profitable. Furthermore, consumer preferences can evolve over time, meaning that market segments are not always stable. Consequently, businesses must continuously analyze market trends and adjust their strategies to remain competitive in changing environments.
