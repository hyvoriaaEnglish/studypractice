Dinosaurs: Rulers of the Ancient World

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the Earth for more than 160 million years. They first appeared during the Triassic Period, approximately 230 million years ago, and evolved into a wide variety of species. Some dinosaurs, such as Tyrannosaurus rex, were powerful predators, while others, including Triceratops and Brachiosaurus, were herbivores. Their remarkable adaptations allowed them to thrive in numerous environments across the planet.

Despite their long period of success, most dinosaurs became extinct around 66 million years ago. The prevailing scientific theory suggests that a massive asteroid struck the Earth, triggering dramatic environmental changes. Dust and debris released into the atmosphere likely blocked sunlight, disrupted food chains, and caused widespread extinction. Although non-avian dinosaurs disappeared, scientists believe that modern birds evolved from certain dinosaur species. Consequently, dinosaurs continue to fascinate researchers and provide valuable insights into the history of life on Earth.
