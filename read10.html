Existential Crisis: When People Lose Their Sense of Direction

An existential crisis is a period during which individuals question the meaning, purpose, or value of their lives. It often occurs during major life transitions, such as entering adulthood, changing careers, or experiencing significant personal loss. During these periods, people may feel uncertain about their goals, identity, or future. Although such feelings can be distressing, psychologists suggest that existential crises are a natural part of human development and self-reflection.

While an existential crisis may initially create anxiety and confusion, it can also lead to personal growth. By confronting difficult questions about their values and aspirations, individuals may gain a deeper understanding of themselves and develop a clearer sense of purpose. However, if feelings of hopelessness or detachment persist for an extended period, professional support may be beneficial. Consequently, many psychologists view existential crises not merely as periods of suffering but as opportunities for transformation and self-discovery.
